From The Orillia Packet and Times, Thursday, February 16, 1933
Walter Perring
The death of Mr Walter Perring occurred at the home of his daughter, Mrs. H. Mungham, 266 Millard Street, on Friday, February 3, 1933, in his eighty-second year. Mr. Perring was a native of England, having been born near Bishop Stafford, Hertfordshire, England and was married in England to Miss Annie Bester, who predeceased him five years ago. They came to Canada in the spring of 1907 and made their home in Orillia. Three daughters and two sons remain, Mrs. H. Mungham and Mrs. H. Bolton, Orillia; Mrs. J. C. Oldfield, Severn Bridge; James Perring, in London, England, and Quartermaster Sergeant A. E. Perring, of the 2nd East Yorks, at Merruit, India. The funeral was held on Monday afternoon from the home of Mrs. Mungham to St. James's cemetery. The service was conducted by the Rev. W. J. Carson, and the address was given by Mr. D. H. Church. the pallbearers were four grandsons, Messrs. Thomas, James, Vern and Leonard Mungham, and two of Mr. Perring's favourite hymns were sung during the service, "Safe in the Arms of Jesus," and "Abide with Me."
