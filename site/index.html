UK Items
UK Census
  1. Check to see if your library has a subscription to Ancestry. If so you may be able to search these census online. You will be able to see transcriptions as well as the original scans.
  2. Check FreeCEN to see if your area has been indexed yet. This project aims to provide a "free-to-view" online searchable database of the 19th century UK census returns.
  3. Search the Internet, some people have transcribed bits and pieces of areas that they are interested in.

UK Births, Marriages, Deaths
  • Births/Christenings/Marriages from 1500's on: use the IGI (International Genealogical Index) at FamilySearch.org International Genealogical Index. This is a partial index, some areas are well covered others are not. Use England IGI Batch Numbers to limit your search to "extracted" records only, sorted by county. These are transcriptions so once you find a record you are interested in you will need to check the original parish records to confirm accuracy. Be very wary of "submitted" records, they can be submitted by anyone and are not checked for accuracy by anyone other than the submitter - checking will be up to you.
  • Baptism/Marriage/Burial before and after 1837: use FreeREG - an ongoing project, baptism, marriage, and burial records, which have been transcribed from parish and non-conformist church registers in the UK.

  • England and Wales, Births/Marriages/Deaths from 1837 on: use FreeBMD. an ongoing project, transcribing the Civil Registration index.
  • Births/Marriages/Deaths from 1837 on: view Ancestry's BMD index scans
  • Ireland, Civil Registraton Indexes (births, 1864-1958, marriages, 1845-1958, and deaths, 1864-1958) familysearch.org
  • UKBMD - links to web sites that offer on-line transcriptions of UK births, marriages, deaths and censuses

  • Lancashire BMD
  • Middlesex England Parish Marriage Records - between 1563 and 1895.

    • Online Parish Clerk Projects (OPCs) - Link to Index of Sites
  • Lancashire

    • Cemetary/Burial Records
  • Manchester City Council, Burial Records Search
  • deceased online - free to search, pay for more details, growing database, holding records mainly from the 1850s onwards
UK Locations - Streets, Towns, Counties
  • Do you have the name of a town? Index of Places in England and Wales - this index shows the county and registration district in which it was situated during the years 1837-1930.
  • Do you have the name of a county? Counties of England and Wales prior to the 1974 boundry changes.
  • Do you want to see a county map? England: County Maps
  • Do you want to find a parish? ParLoc, UK Parish Locator program - This Windows program enables you to locate any one of over 15,000 UK parishes. It will produce a list of parishes in any County or a list of parishes within a given radius of any other parish.
  • Do you have the name of a street or a place? Streetmap.co.uk - current street maps
  • Do you have a London street name? Victorian London A-Z Street Index
  • A History of the County of Middlesex: Volume 5 - Hendon, Kingsbury, Great Stanmore, Little Stanmore, Edmonton Enfield, Monken Hadley, South Mimms, Tottenham (1976)

Canadian Items
  • Canadian Genealogy Centre - Library and Archives Canada
  • Soldiers of the First World War; Canadian Expeditionary Force - Over 600,000 Canadians enlisted in the Canadian Expeditionary Force (CEF) during the First World War. The CEF database is an index to those personnel files, which are held by the National Archives. I found my grandfather and great uncle in here.
  • Second World War Service Files: Canadian Armed Forces War Dead
Census & Directories
  • 1881 Canadian Census - SEARCHABLE - via www.familysearch.org
  • 1911 Census of Canada Indexing Project - Searchable by surname; has scans
  • Census of Canada, 1911 - Searchable by geographic location only; has scans
  • Our Roots - full text of Canadian local histories in French and English
  • Directories, Photos, Newspapers : Peel's Prairie Provinces - from the University of Alberta
Home Children, Emigration, etc
  • Young Immigrants to Canada - Marjorie Kohli from the University of Waterloo has been researching Barnardo children. My grandmother Alice Latham as well as her brother Albert were Barnardo children.
  • Home Children (1869-1930) - Searchable! Library and Archives Canada - "Members of the British Isles Family History Society of Greater Ottawa are locating and indexing the names of these Home Children found in passenger lists in the custody of the National Archives of Canada."
  • Passenger Lists, 1865-1922
